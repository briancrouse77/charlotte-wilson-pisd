About Charlotte

Charlotte Wilson didn't just read about the challenges inside Princeton ISD classrooms — she lived them as a special education teacher in the district. She knows firsthand where students are falling through the cracks.

Her Platform

Five Pillars for Princeton's Students

These aren't talking points — they're classroom realities Charlotte witnessed as a PISD teacher.

⚖️

Campus Equity

Princeton's school buildings may look the same from the outside — but inside, resources, staffing, and programs are not distributed equally across campuses. Charlotte will push for every classroom, not just some, to be fully supported.

🎓

Bilingual & Special Education

As a former special ed teacher, Charlotte knows that bilingual learners and students with disabilities deserve qualified staff and proper resources — not waiting lists and workarounds. She'll hold the board accountable for real, measurable improvements.

🔎

Independent Audit

Charlotte is calling for an independent audit of PISD's technology, resources, and special programs. Princeton taxpayers deserve to know where their dollars are going — and whether they're reaching every student equally.

📋

Transparency & Public Records

Public business should be conducted in public. Charlotte will advocate for all board meeting records — including minutes from 2022, 2023, and 2024 — to be easily accessible to every Princeton resident who asks.

🤝

Year-Round Community Engagement

A board member who only shows up at election time isn't serving the community. Charlotte commits to sustained engagement with PTOs, faith communities, local businesses, and families — 365 days a year.

Accountability Watch

Questions Princeton Deserves Answers To

During the 2025 campaign, Charlotte raised documented, specific concerns about district decisions. These questions were not fully addressed by the board — and the community still deserves answers.

🏛️

TASB → TEE & TFER: Who Decided?

Princeton ISD quietly moved away from the Texas Association of School Boards (TASB) — the established, widely-trusted state organization — toward Texans for Excellence in Education (TEE) and the Texas Foundation for Education Rights (TFER).

These newer groups have been associated with advocacy for charter school expansion. The shift happened without public community input. When Charlotte raised this at the 2025 candidate forum, the board compared it to choosing between "Coke and Pepsi" — without addressing the underlying policy implications.

The question remains: Who authorized this change, when, and what was the community's role in that decision?
📄

Where Are the Meeting Minutes?

Charlotte alleged that PISD board meeting minutes for 2022, 2023, and 2024 were not publicly accessible when she raised the issue during her 2025 campaign.

Under Texas law, public school board meetings must be open, and records must be available to residents upon request. The Texas Open Meetings Act is clear on this obligation.

What you can do: Submit a public records request to PISD at open-records-request@princetonisd.net or contact Collin County Elections for guidance.
Note: These are issues raised during the documented 2025 campaign and reported by the Princeton Herald. Charlotte's team presents them as open questions for the community — not as established conclusions. Residents are encouraged to research and engage directly with the district.
